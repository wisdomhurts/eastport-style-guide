01
Foundation

A deposit is only worth what the market can understand.

Eastport is a critical-minerals developer advancing five projects in Botswana. This system is the layer between that geology and the capital markets — investor-grade, disciplined, and built for scrutiny.

Positioning

Capital meets discipline.

We present a diversified, advanced-stage portfolio — copper, nickel, cobalt, uranium and rare earths — to the institutional and retail investors who fund it. Every map, deck and page is held to the standard of a public-company news release.

Premium is a function of restraint. The work should read like an institutional research note: calm authority, evidence over adjectives, numbers that carry the argument.

Register

Confident and quiet. Structure comes from white space and 1px hairlines — not color blocks or ornament.

Discipline

Cool neutrals carry the weight. Azure is earned — it marks the one action, the live tick, never decoration.

Audience

Portfolio managers and analysts; geologists and GIS specialists; the retail investor reading the deck.

One thesis, repeated. Five projects · ~$20M invested · 100+ years of combined experience · a lean, heavily-aligned team in a tier-one jurisdiction. Every surface should ladder back to that. If a page doesn't advance the thesis, cut it.

Investor-grade. Specific. Never hype.

We write like a public-company news release crossed with a research note. Confidence comes from evidence and numbers — not adjectives. Sentence case; tickers in mono caps.

Words we use

Words we avoid

No exclamation marks. No emoji, ever. Avoid superlatives a regulator would flag.

Five critical metals projects, one disciplined developer.

Capital meets discipline.

Casing & tickers. Sentence case for headlines and CTAs. Title Case is fine for asset names (Matsitama Copper Project, Nakalakwana Hill). Tickers always in mono caps and paired the way the Company writes them: TSXV:EVI · OTCQB:EVIIF. Units stay precise: 1,845 km² · 50,000 m · CAD $20M · NI 43-101.

09
Motion & Moments

Atmosphere, not decoration.

Two signature motifs — the radiating fan and the spectrum rule — used sparingly and quietly. Motion is calm and functional: short ease-out fades, a settling press, no bounce.

The dawn fan

An oversized, low-opacity mark behind a hero or section header — monochrome blue, subtle, never loud.

The spectrum rule

The 5-blue gradient as a 2–6px line — under the nav, atop the footer, capping a hero. The one decorative flourish.

Do
Fade and rise content 6–10px on entry. Lift cards −4px into the deep shadow on hover. Keep durations 0.16–0.4s, ease-out. Gate everything behind prefers-reduced-motion.
Don't
No bounce, spring or overshoot. No infinite looping decoration on content. Never animate the logo's blades apart. Never let motion delay the data an investor came for.

Legible to everyone, by default.

Contrast, focus and reduced motion are part of the system, not an afterthought — fitting for a public company that answers to scrutiny.

Focus

A 2px Azure outline at 2px offset on every interactive element; form fields add a 3px azure glow. Never remove focus styles.

Reduced motion

All reveals, the cover rays and the scroll cue collapse to static under prefers-reduced-motion: reduce.

Muted text floor

Mute #93A0B0 is the lightest text allowed on white — and only for meta. Never use Line or Mist as text.

Don't rely on color

Commodity and stage are always paired with a label, never color alone — so a legend reads without hue.
